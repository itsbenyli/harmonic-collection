Falling as Rain

I imagine myself as a single drop of rain, released from a cloud, plunging through the sky with a silvered gleam. I feel the wind rushing past, the sudden shift from freedom to gravity, tracing arcs across the world below. Each surface I touch ripples outward, leaving a fleeting mark, a whisper of presence before I dissolve into something greater.

I glance over rooftops and streets, noticing reflections of neon and sunlight, the patterns of puddles collecting the fragments of life. Each landing is soft, brief, yet transformative—a small dialogue with the earth.

Landing on a leaf, I tremble with tension, feeling its texture, its resistance, its quiet strength. Tiny world within a droplet, a mirror of movement, light, and life.

Finally I join the river of water on the streets, merging with others, a symphony of droplets cascading together, blending the sky’s release with the pulse of the city below. Every drop adds to the rhythm, the cycle, the endless reflection of being.
