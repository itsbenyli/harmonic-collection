Through the Mirror of Dreams

I move through the world as if through a softened mirror, edges bending, light drifting, colors loosening into haze. There is always that brief moment before waking— the second where I am not myself yet, where memory and imagination overlap like two silhouettes trying to become one.

As I walk through this shifting surface, I see versions of myself reflected back— some younger, some quieter, some still reaching for things I’ve already let go of. They linger in the glass the way dreams do: not quite real, not quite forgotten, hovering in the space between becoming and being.

Stepping forward feels like moving deeper into the mirror, every gesture revealing a little more of the figure behind the fog. My reflection sharpens slowly, not because it becomes real, but because I finally allow myself to look.

By the time I reach the end, the dream no longer blurs— it folds into the shape of the present, quiet, steady, whole.
