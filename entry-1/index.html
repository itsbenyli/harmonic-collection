Coffee

Every morning begins with the same ritual, the warmth between my hands, the slow curl of steam, the first bitter breath. Coffee isn’t just a drink; it is a mirror of rhythm and patience, a pause that lets the day begin with awareness.

The aroma rises slowly, mingling with the morning air. Each sip marks a gentle beginning, a reminder that simple rituals carry the weight of our memories.

The liquid glimmers in the sunlight as it dances inside the cup. Shadows and light perform an unspoken choreography.

The first sunlight filtering through the window touches the coffee surface like a gentle brushstroke.

My mother’s hands, always steady, always quiet, stirring sugar three times. I trace her gestures in mine.
