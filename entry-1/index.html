Taste of Coffee

Bitterness is never just about flavor. The first sip holds a small resistance, a faint sting that wakes the senses. As warmth spreads, it turns softer, almost kind. It reminds me that emotions too can taste bitter before they become understanding.

The sunlight falls through the kitchen window and touches the surface of my coffee. Light and liquid blend into a brief shimmer, like time itself melting into warmth. Every morning, I watch this small performance of light and think about how beauty often hides in repetition.
