Window

The window frames the world like a painting, each glance a composition of light, shadow, and motion. Outside, life moves in its unplanned rhythm, and inside, I observe quietly, noticing patterns in ordinary moments. A window is both barrier and invitation, separating and connecting at once.

The frame captures colors, textures, and fleeting gestures. Light refracts across the glass, highlighting dust motes and revealing details normally unnoticed. Each time I look, the scene shifts, reminding me that perception is alive, dynamic, and deeply personal.

Raindrops streak down the glass in irregular patterns, blurring and distorting the world outside. I watch the city dissolve into a soft abstraction. Each drop mirrors fleeting thoughts, merging memory with present emotion. Rain makes the ordinary feel intimate, a reminder of impermanence and reflection.
